In the following chart we see that our monthly sales all grouped together regardless of burger type or geographical location. As is shown there is a downward trend that occured with the introduction of the Impossible burger on October 2018. But since then there has been some growth in areas.

In the following 5 charts we can see that our monthly sales are divided by geographical locations based on area. As is shown basically all locations had a dip in sales after the introduction of the impossible burger in October 2018 with hamburgers taking the biggest hit in all locations but the SouthEast. Interestingly, that is the only area where hamburgers are not the best selling burger but Fish Filet is. But even though Fish Filet is the number one burger it also had lower sales starting October 2018. But even though there was downward spike some areas have recoevred and shown growth.

In the following 3 charts we can see that our monthly sales are divided by burger type. As is shown in the other charts there was a dip in all burger sales on October 2018. We see that all burger sales are at lower levels than their highs in August 2018, right before the introduction of the impossible burger. But while hamburger sales seem to be on downward trend, we can see that chicken filets are showing soming growth and fish flet sales are increasing rapidly.

In conclusion, based on the data it seems that the Impossible burger did put a dent in all burger sales. And while it seems that some chicken filet and many fish filet sales are recovering, making a viable alternative to the Impossible burger might help enourage growth in all sales, but especially the best seller, the hamburger.
